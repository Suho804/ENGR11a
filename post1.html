Prusa MINI+ 3D Printer

The printer came with pre-assembled parts so we didn't have to build it from scratch. It came with a guidebook we could follow and blueprint measurements for checking the length or the name of the parts. The step was simple and consistent, it also came with a gummy bear to boost our motivation from time to time.

Calibration

After the machine was assembled we had to calibrate the x, y, and z points for the machine nozzle to find the extreme point when printing. It was done by selecting 'First layer' testing print in the printer setting. We have tried 4 times to make sure the print was not too thin or too thick.
Our final calibration point was 0.9933 and to test the margin of error we printed 'Benchy'

Reflection

Building the printer, calibrating it, printing to see precision, and expanding the printer program by modifying the print setting was a great experience. I learned that certain settings are fixed to decrease errors and that there is different printer setter depending on different types of filament. Building the printer as the instructions said was easy to follow, however, calibrating to have the exact print thickness of filament was a consistent test of trial and error.
The things I would change are...
- Having a separate instruction paper add in the same instruction paper on the dicidded same page to reduce confusion.
- Have a more transparent tube and inserter to be more visible to check with ease when inserting the filament.

Benchy 1

This is the first Benchy, and it took 4 hours and 49 minutes to print. Through the '3dbenchy' website I was able to measure Benchy 1 dimensions and look for any changes in calibration settings.

Bridge roof length: 22.88
Chimney roundness: 2.97 & 6.88
Horizontal overall length: 61.88
Horizontal overall width: 30.96
Vertical overall-height: 15.49 & 48.08
Cargo-box size: 11.98 x 10.81 & 8.04 x 6.95
Hawsepipe diameter: 4.00
Bridge front window size: 10.68 x 9.18
Bridge rear window size: 8.97 x 11.92
Overall, the error margin is about +/- 1

Benchy 2

This is the second Benchy, and it took 1 hour 34 minutes to print. I have modified the print setting in my computer with the program 'Pursa Slicer' to decrease the overall time it took to print than the original print setting.
I have optimized the print speed movement to be much faster than the original printing speed and decreased the infill by 5%. I couldn't change the thickness of the filament or the size of Benchy to reduce the time.
