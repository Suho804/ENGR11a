This is the final outcome of the Alarm clock kit, Further assembly image on the side widget.

Solder the alarm clock kit

Soldering the circuit board: Using soldering wire and soldering iron connect the resistors, capacitor, resistor network, etc.. on the board by inserting them through the hole.
The key is to place the soldering iron horizontally and apply the wire vertically for ease.
It is very important to solder each individual while they are not touching each other.
Check the time on the alarm clock to see if the electric circuit is all working well.

Design plan

These represent several crucial elements and characteristics that a well-designed alarm clock should incorporate. It is essential to consider various aspects, including overall measurements such as the dimensions of the case, circuit board, and battery. Additionally, you should carefully evaluate the shape and frame of the case, the design of the battery holder, and the type of joints used in the assembly, with all measurements provided in millimeters. Our primary objective in creating this alarm clock has been to ensure a design that is both straightforward and highly efficient.

Trials of Alarm clock Cases

1st trial(Yellow_Green) was too tight with a thickness of 1mm, which caused the protective case to bend flexibly. The position of the window frame for the number was too far left.
2nd trial(Dark_Green) was halfway printed when it encountered an error. This time the thickness was adjusted to 6mm and the windows were shifted to the right, the infill pattern was swift however, the filament ended at the right corner.

Measurements & Reflection

This is the picture evidence of a measuring circuit board with digital calipers. The slicer setting is set to default setting with the printer setting 0.20 mm speed. Through the slicer setting layers, I was able to identify because it wasn't printed built in it may have had a +/- error on the thickness of the hole or the joint.
The pink case(Final outcome) has a tight joint that doesn't go in smoothly, my partner had to file down 6 joints on each side 24 times to make it fit. The buttons were printed 2 times: the first button was orange and the second button was bright pink. the length of the button was lacking on the first button and because we didn't have super glue we used hot glue to join the head and the rod together. As a result, due to the rod having a small surface area for it to push, my partner improvised by adding the head on the end of each rod providing a larger surface area and length.
