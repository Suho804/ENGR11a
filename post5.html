MDF Alarm clock case

This is the final outcome of the laser-cut finger joint alarm clock cover. It was generated by modifying it with Fusion 360 and conducting a cutting analysis using Lightburn software.

Measurement sketch

The previous alarm clock cover I made using 3D printing had large tolerances and the cover face holes were slanted right. For this project, I used the dimension of the exemplary laser-cut cover design. Using the 'change parameter' I was able to use standard length for all dimension that was needed, and if there was a change needed I only had to change the number value on the 'change parameter' therefore, it was easy to make changes.

MDF measurement consideration

I have made the measurement according to the standard side of the circuit board, and because the thickness of the material (MDF) is 3mm the length of the joint should be 3mm to display a clean edge for the joints. Therefore, I had to add both 3mm on the side of the box.
The cover displays the time through 3 windows on the cover face, there are 3 holes on the bottom for the user to push the buttons, the sound will come through the wall of the cover face, and the cover face can be lifted upward to replace the battery. The other finger joints have been glued together to provide sturdyness.

LightBurn software

This is the screenshot from the light burn software. The grid is x = 450 and y = 700 for the laser printer size, the speed is 20(mm/s) and the power is 100(%).
I have placed the sketches, and DFX files imported from Fusion 360, at the end of the top left corner in order to minimize the waste produced from the laser cutting. Also, the file was cut by combining 4 ~ 5 people's sketches to maximize the cutting process.

Flat layout of the design

This is the top view from the flat layout of the design. During the process, I modified three types of design to have the lowest tolerances overall for the cover face.
Refer to Side Widget
I focused my process on having a first, good fit on the overall cover case dimension. Therefore, I printed the first prototype with a large window as the cover face. As the configuration was secure, I focused on finding the right dimension for the holes to be present. This took 2 trials of error, as my third product is my final successful outcome.
The difficulty of using a laser cutter is to continuously check the dimension and position of the sketch to make sure it is not overlapped. In order to prevent the mistake I used 'display component color' I was able to show separate components if they were overlapping each other or not.
