Process Portfolio for Record

Final outcome of the product

This is the combination of two existing design from thingiverese: iPhone 12 case and fidget herringbone gear, joined by modifying the back of the case.

3D printed modified iPhone case

This is the print setting of the Prusa slicer for iPhone 12 and 2 Herringbone Gears. I have made 2 snap joints for the fidget gear to be attached to the phone providing the purpose of a phone stand/phone grip The outcome of the design was successful, the snap joint and the gear fitted well, and it was able to perform as a phone stander as well.

In the first print, I printed it horizontally, but after it was printed when I applied force to push the gear attach it broke in the direction of the filament layer unable to withstand the force

Disassembly of Fidget gear

This is the print setting of the Prusa slicer for fidget gear connections. I have disassembled the original design by slicing the frame in half and adding the thread feature in fusion for it to securely join together when the herringbone gear is placed in.

I first printed the fidget gear to see the functions, however, I noticed that it was all stuck, and it broke when I applied too much force. ideaMaker picture reference on the right side widget.

Herringbone gear connection function Result

This is the second version of the gear connections, the method using thread helped to have a secured joint, but the process of tightening it until the end was difficult.
It may be due to the unevenness of print that limits the thread edges to join together smoothly.

Reflection

Sketching: I had to think of an way to optimize the gear which I first print by adding a object, perfomring new purpose. I sketched the function and purpose of the object if I combined a phone case further in my sketch.

CAD: Using fusion 360 I imported two exising products, fidget gear & iPhone case, as a mesh. Then by converting to soild body I modified the phone case by adding two snap joint rod and also modified the gear connector to have screw joint; developed version from my first print.

CAM: Using Prusa Slicer I have changed the setting to optimize the print. I have decresed the infill as much as posible as both of the design don't need hard structure, I have added a brim to have a clean print and focused the support material.

Result: Some of the issue that I have overcomed was checking the printer name setting, printing first layer print for cheking consistency of the printer, choosing the position to print when printing somthing that has to put some tension on.

Further design: I would reduce the surface area of the phone case to reduce print filiament, by making it optimize the web shape. I would print the product imbedded to reduce errror in measurement.
