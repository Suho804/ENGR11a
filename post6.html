Puzzle for visually impared children

Puzzle game for visually impaired children that is inclusive and educational at the same time. The puzzle is about learning how street road works; how the road is connected and what formation the cross road has, along with how buildings are layout. There will be street pieces, decore pieces, and stop signs/lamp lights.

Customer Profile

Goal: To learn and socialize with other friends by using puzzles.
Age: 6 - 10 years old
Job: Children who are visually impaired
Location: Massachusetts
Challenges: Need education in braille and socializing
Environment: Facility where they provide services for visually impaired people
BIO: A child learning braille and spatial objects through collaboration with other students who are learning alphabet and object identification.

Emailing Process

Oct.31 We have emailed Dina Rosenbaum, Chief Program Officer at the Carroll Center for the Blind. Their response was that they are not a school therefore the educators who work with children are out in the field delivering itinerant services. And their students are extremely diverse in their learning and abilities. They gave the direct email address of Nikc, who is the Accessibility and Research Coordinator at the Carroll Center. With him, we made an appointment to talk about plans for tactile puzzles for the blind. A possible way to have it reinforce other concepts that they teach in rehabilitation services(Braille) The following meeting was through Zoom (On Nov 2), we prepared specific questions to ask the members

List of Questions to ask in the first meeting
1. Could you share any feedback or input from visually impaired students or their parents regarding their preferences or needs for educational materials?
2. What services do you currently provide to children? How are the Braille events taught?
3. What are some successes or challenges you(faculty/educator) or the children face while they try to learn Braille?
4. What level of challenge would children like when solving the puzzle? (Psycho pleasure) - develop more for entertainment (scrabble) or education (fish puzzle)

Defining the problem

Visually impaired children have a hard time comprehending the real object and socializing with sighted children.

5 potential ways to solve the problem
1. Farm mapping(3D print animal and have textures/sounds)
2. Road map
3. Maze
4. State map(Massachusetts map)
5. Chess game

Final chosen product: A road map

First Meeting

Proposal:
* Prefer using our product as part of the curriculum, not just for one child
* Possible collaboration with Perkins School for the Blind(We have appointment on Nov 11th)

Important aspects they mentioned
1. Literacy(Braille)
2. Spatial tactile way to learn
For spatial ability, a Blind child tends to not know the full composition of the object.

The key question they asked
How is Puzzle going to expand the perception of the world?

Tactile people learn through basic touch, the prototype of the fish is a great starting point, however, it should tie to the relevance of what a vision would tell people. Therefore, Exposure and experience are important to learn about the world.
Realistic example are a map of the campus(intergrade braille in 4-way interaction and format), a Map of the streets, Map of the farm(the client lack the understanding of spatial perception of the animal, thus tend to be more afraid)

Inclusivity
If we make a puzzle that is ONLY for visually impaired children, they are going to feel exclusive from their peers, therefore, a game that would bridge blind and sighted children is preferred.

Another possible problem to investigate
Challenging to navigate the website, a tactical indication of how the website is formed would allow the child to explore more information around the world.
